Purpose of the appointment discussion

This is not a request for a prescription or a claim that the system already works. It is a measured rehabilitation project for a chronic visual-field deficit after stroke. I would like the doctor's view on medical safety, useful clinical measurements, and whether the assumptions make sense from a physician's point of view.

1. One-Minute Summary

Condition

Left homonymous hemianopia after a right PCA stroke in July 2021, with persistent left visual-field loss and severe left contrast deficit.

Project

NegletFix uses VR to deliver repeated audiovisual training in the affected left field, while measuring contrast sensitivity and functional change over time.

Doctor Input Needed

Confirm safety, suggest clinical outcome measures, review fatigue/medication/lab issues, and flag any contraindication before disciplined training starts.

The clean medical framing

NegletFix is best understood as an N-of-1, measurement-first rehabilitation protocol. The first goal is not to prove a cure. The first goal is to run a repeatable, low-risk training block and objectively determine whether Eric's left hemifield detection, contrast sensitivity, reaction time, or daily function changes beyond measurement noise.

3. Baseline Measurement Already Obtained

NegletFix includes a modified Pelli-Robson-style contrast sensitivity test. A critical positioning bug was fixed before the valid baseline below: letters now appear in the intended hemifield while Eric fixates a central cross.

Hemifield LogCS result Approx. threshold Interpretation
Central ~1.05+ ~9 percent contrast Usable central baseline; useful as a display/fatigue calibration check.
Right / intact field 2.25 ~0.6 percent contrast Excellent; maxed out the current test.
Left / affected field 0.00 100 percent contrast not detected Severe deficit; pure black-on-gray letters were not identified in the affected hemifield.

Asymmetry: 2.25 LogCS, far above the usual clinically meaningful change threshold of about 0.30 LogCS. The plan is to compare future results against this baseline, not against population norms from a different instrument.

Measurement caveat

The NegletFix contrast test is currently best used for within-device longitudinal tracking. LCD/OLED contrast measurements can differ from calibrated printed charts. That is acceptable for this project if the same device, brightness, distance, and fixation rules are kept stable.

4. Scientific Rationale

The working hypothesis is that even when the primary visual cortex pathway is damaged, some residual visual processing can be trained through alternate pathways and repeated task practice.

Concept Why it matters for NegletFix Confidence
Two visual pathways The V1/geniculostriate pathway is damaged for the left field, but retinotectal and extrastriate pathways may still support orienting, motion, and partial detection. high anatomy
Audiovisual pairing Spatially and temporally congruent sound plus visual cues can strengthen detection in weak visual fields; this is the core of the training. moderate clinical
Chronic stroke reality Eric is almost 5 years post-stroke. Recovery is still possible in some chronic patients, but effect sizes are expected to be modest and variable. mixed
Daily-function transfer The most important question is whether lab gains transfer to reading, walking, screens, cooking, navigation, and fatigue. This must be tracked directly. unknown

5. Research Findings We Are Using

Evidence branch What it suggests How NegletFix uses it
Daibert-Nido et al. 2021 A small pediatric hemianopia pilot reported visual-field and contrast-sensitivity gains after home immersive audiovisual training. Useful proof-of-concept, but not enough by itself because it was not a powered adult-stroke trial.
Misawa / Daibert-Nido 2024 A home telerehabilitation version using Meta Quest 2/Pro was feasible in pediatric hemianopia. Supports the practical idea that a Quest-based home protocol can be usable, monitored, and repeated.
Alharshan / Alwashmi 2026 Fifteen subacute/chronic stroke patients with HH completed 30 min/day, 5 days/week, for 6 weeks, with behavioral and imaging changes reported. This is the closest direct dose model for Eric: 30 sessions over 6 weeks, about 15 total hours.
Namgung et al. 2025 A randomized clinical trial of digital visual perceptual learning in post-stroke visual field defects found measurable visual-field improvements. Supports the broader premise that structured digital training can produce measurable chronic post-stroke visual gains in some patients.
Yang / Saionz / Huxlin line Chronic V1-damage patients can improve, but contrast sensitivity remains fundamentally limited and not everyone responds. Sets realistic expectations: a clinically meaningful gain would be valuable, but full normalization is not the target.
ESO 2025 visual impairment guideline Standardized screening and access to vision rehabilitation after stroke are important; the evidence base is still uneven. Supports asking for formal measurement/referral rather than relying only on self-report.

6. Planned NegletFix Protocol

Proposed first real training block

30 minutes per day, 5 days per week, for 6 weeks if tolerated. This mirrors the closest adult-stroke audiovisual training dose found in the recent literature. Start conservatively if VR fatigue or nausea appears.

Protocol element Implementation Reason
Training stimulus Co-localized visual target plus auditory cue in VR, primarily presented toward the affected left field. Uses multisensory pairing to help weak visual signals become behaviorally useful.
Difficulty progression Starts near the scotoma border and adjusts contrast/eccentricity based on performance. Severe left baseline means starting too far into the blind field would produce only failure and fatigue.
Feedback Open-loop task feedback first. EEG is not required for the first protocol. Keeps the first training block simpler and testable.
EEG / Muse layer Exploratory only. Validate signal quality before using it as a training gate. Consumer EEG evidence is mixed; it should not be the foundation of the first medical discussion.
Data logging Each trial logs eccentricity, hemifield, contrast, reaction time, hit/miss, and audiovisual timing. Allows the project to learn from actual data rather than memory or impression.

8. Outcome Measures To Discuss

Measure How to use it Why it matters
NegletFix contrast sensitivity Central + left quick-check every 5 sessions; full central/right/left reassessment at baseline and post-program. Primary project measurement.
Formal visual field test Humphrey, Goldmann, or local equivalent if available. Clinically recognizable external anchor for the doctor file.
Reaction time and hit rate From the VR task logs at specific eccentricities. May improve before conscious contrast detection improves.
Daily-function tracker Short weekly ratings: screen fatigue, cooking, navigation, left-side collisions/surprises, reading, evening exhaustion. Prevents the project from chasing a lab number that does not help daily life.
Fatigue / sleep / mood / medication review Review during routine care and prescription renewal. Training requires vigilance; sedation, poor sleep, depression, or uncontrolled vascular risk can bury any signal.

9. Questions For Dr. Jacquemin

  1. Does the medical framing make sense? Chronic left homonymous hemianopia after right PCA stroke, with a measured contrast-sensitivity asymmetry.
  2. Is there any reason VR audiovisual training would be unsafe for me? Especially seizure history, migraines, vestibular symptoms, severe fatigue, blood pressure, or photosensitivity.
  3. Should I get formal visual-field testing before the training block? If yes, what test and through whom: ophthalmology, neuro-ophthalmology, orthoptist, or rehabilitation clinic?
  4. Should we distinguish hemianopia from neglect more formally? NegletFix started from "neglect" language, but my documented problem is homonymous hemianopia. If attention/neglect is also present, that changes the interpretation.
  5. What blood work or prescription review matters for training? Lipids, HbA1c/glucose, CBC, CMP/electrolytes, B12, vitamin D, TSH, blood pressure, sleep, fatigue, and medication side effects may affect rehabilitation capacity.
  6. Would you be willing to review pre/post results later? The useful doctor role may be to sanity-check whether the data look meaningful or only noise.
  7. Do you see any specialist referral that would make this safer or more rigorous? Neuro-ophthalmology, OT/orthoptics, stroke rehab, or visual rehabilitation specialist.

10. Safety Boundaries

Not a substitute

This does not replace medical care, vascular prevention, eye exams, formal rehab, or physician-directed treatment.

Not driving clearance

Any functional improvement in VR is not permission to drive. Driving decisions require formal legal and medical assessment.

Stop rules

Pause training if it provokes major headache, vertigo, nausea, unusual neurological symptoms, visual distress, or marked fatigue.

11. Side Question: Hyperbaric Oxygen

Why this is only a side note

Eric remembered hyperbaric oxygen therapy from a recovery podcast and noticed that the practice may have a hyperbaric module. After a quick evidence check, this does not look like the main NegletFix topic.

HBOT is real medicine, but stroke recovery is not a standard accepted indication. Acute ischemic stroke guidelines do not recommend HBOT except for stroke caused by air embolization. Chronic-stroke studies exist, but I did not find strong direct evidence for chronic post-PCA homonymous hemianopia or left-field contrast restoration.

If Dr. Jacquemin thinks HBOT is relevant anyway, the safe framing would be: off-label adjunct, clear contraindication screening, and pre/post measurement. It should not replace the core NegletFix protocol.

HBOT discussion point Question for doctor
Relevance Do you see any plausible role in my chronic visual-field condition, or would you consider it unrelated/unproven?
Safety Any contraindications in my history: ears/sinuses, lungs, seizure risk, eye history, glucose, medications, blood pressure?
Measurement If ever considered, should it wait until after a clean baseline NegletFix block so we do not confuse what caused any change?
