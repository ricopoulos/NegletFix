# Crumb: Cardio As Brain-State / Training-Tolerance Branch

**Date**: 2026-05-30

## Context

While discussing Recovery After Stroke episode 406, Eric connected with the cardio / endorphin theme. This confirmed a prior conversation with his ex-cardiologist about weight loss and **30 minutes of real walking per day**.

Eric had previously watched his Apple Watch heart rate during walks and felt afraid when it rose into the **120-140 bpm** range because heart rate, blood pressure, and stroke risk felt emotionally linked. His ex-cardiologist told him not to be afraid of exercise heart-rate rise in his condition and said even around **160 bpm** was not dangerous for him.

## Interpretation

This is not a NegletFix protocol change. It is a future research/measurement branch:

- Cardio may be relevant as a **brain/body state variable**: alertness, fatigue tolerance, mood, sleep, vascular health, endorphins, and possible BDNF/neuroplasticity signaling.
- It should not be framed as "cardio restores the visual field."
- Better framing: safe, gentle aerobic activity may improve the conditions that make a 30-session VR training block easier to complete and recover from.

## Guardrail

Do not prescribe an exercise program from this note. Use clinician guidance, especially with Eric's stroke history and any vascular/cardiac medications. The useful next step is to discuss safe target intensity and stop criteria with his doctor, then track consistently if approved.
