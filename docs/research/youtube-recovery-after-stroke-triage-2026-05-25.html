404. She Couldn't Recognise Faces for 7 Years. One Session Fixed It.

QEEG-guided laser / photobiomodulation lead added after Eric's note.

Medium

NegletFix relevance: The useful part is not the dramatic "one session" claim. The useful part is the workflow idea: use QEEG as a functional brain map, then use that map to guide where a stimulation or photobiomodulation intervention is aimed. This is adjacent to NegletFix's EEG interest, but it is not the same as Muse TP10 neurofeedback.

1:00Intro frames the topic as QEEG-guided transcranial photobiomodulation using a functional brain map.
10:00The guest describes learning about laser / photobiomodulation and then quantitative EEG as a way to decide where to target it.
11:30Case story: QEEG allegedly guided the first laser treatment, followed by a claimed change in acquired prosopagnosia.
21:00Targeting is compared to identifying vulnerable brain regions and choosing individualized laser parameters.
32:30Distinguishes visual inspection of raw EEG traces from computational pattern analysis.
56:00Guest says QEEG-guided transcranial photobiomodulation is not readily available and would require specialized practitioners.

What QEEG appears to mean here: quantitative EEG: computerized analysis of EEG signals, often summarized as maps of spectral power, frequency, asymmetry, coherence, or other derived measures. It is not a different kind of electrical signal; it is a different way of processing and interpreting EEG.

Verification anchors: ACNS quantitative EEG guideline resources, 2024 review on transcranial photobiomodulation for stroke, and human transcranial photobiomodulation physiology study.

Research status for NegletFix: add to watchlist. Do not alter the current open-loop AV training plan. The question is whether QEEG could become a better future mapping layer than consumer Muse EEG, not whether it replaces the current 30-session protocol.

4. Deep Digest: Episodes 274 and 156

This is the first bridge from survivor interviews to NegletFix design. The point is to identify real-world symptoms and candidate daily metrics that the Unity protocol does not currently capture.

Episode 274: symptom bridge

  • Clinician pathway: the episode repeatedly points toward neuro-ophthalmology / neuro-optometry for brain-origin vision problems when the eye itself appears structurally healthy.
  • Environment triggers: bright sun, overcast glare, crowds, screens, driving, and high-stimulation events appear as practical triggers.
  • Protective behaviors: hat, sunglasses, narrowed visual input, soft gaze, reduced digital exposure, and closing eyes during car travel.
  • Fatigue pattern: overdoing stimulation may worsen deficits; the key distinction is transient next-day settling versus prolonged regression.
  • NegletFix implication: daily function logs should track bright-day tolerance, screen tolerance, visual fatigue, and recovery after stimulation-heavy days.

Episode 156: function bridge

  • Stroke presentation: sudden visual field loss can be mistaken for an eye problem when classic FAST symptoms are absent.
  • Neurovisual framing: the guest separates ocular health from brain interpretation of visual information.
  • Rehab gap: vision-specific therapy may not be automatically prescribed after discharge, unlike PT, OT, speech, or cardiac rehab.
  • Functional domains: reading, figure-ground perception, visual search, midline, driving, rain/night driving, and large-print tasks all appear.
  • NegletFix implication: contrast sensitivity alone is not enough. Add simple self-ratings or tasks for reading effort, visual search, figure-ground difficulty, and post-session fatigue.

Bright / glare tolerance Episode 274 repeatedly connects symptoms with sunny, overcast, and visually intense environments. 0-10 rating before/after training days; note hat/sunglasses use and outdoor exposure.

Driving / navigation confidence Both episodes touch driving or travel impact, but this is safety-sensitive. Self-report only unless clinician-guided. Do not treat app improvement as driving clearance.
Design consequence
The AV training module should remain scientifically narrow, but the research layer should track lived function. A future session log could add five quick checkboxes after each training day: glare, screen, reading, fatigue, and stimulation load.

5. Themes That Matter For NegletFix

Strong themes

  • Neurological vision problems can be missed if the eye itself appears healthy.
  • Bright light, crowds, screens, motion, and complex environments may be major functional triggers.
  • Reading difficulty is a separate practical outcome from detection or driving.
  • Vision-specific rehab is not always provided automatically after stroke.
  • Patient stories can reveal daily-life outcomes that formal papers may under-describe.

Research cautions

  • Most episodes are survivor interviews, not controlled studies.
  • Titles with "fixed it" or treatment breakthroughs should be flagged for verification.
  • Neurofeedback, HBOT, Etanercept, and stem-cell content should not influence the protocol unless independently verified.
  • Vision loss is a broad category; hemianopia, ocular problems, agnosia, reading deficits, and sensory overload are not the same mechanism.
