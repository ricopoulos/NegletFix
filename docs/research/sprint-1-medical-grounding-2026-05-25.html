2. Verified Condition Explanation

This is the clean version to reuse in future NegletFix pages:

Homonymous hemianopia means loss of vision toward the same side of the visual world in both eyes. For Eric, the right occipital/PCA stroke affects the left side of the visual world. The eyes can be structurally normal because the problem is in the brain's visual pathway, not primarily in the eyeballs.

Common daily impacts include missing objects or people on the affected side, difficulty in crowds and crossings, reading problems, mobility hazards, and driving-safety issues. Formal visual field testing and brain imaging are the standard way to identify and localize the problem.

Current mainstream management is mostly supportive/rehabilitative: low-vision or vision-rehabilitation specialists, scanning/search training, environmental adaptation, and sometimes prisms. Restorative field recovery after stroke is most likely early and becomes much less likely in the chronic period, which is why NegletFix measures modest change carefully instead of assuming full restoration.

Claim Verification Status
Same-side field loss occurs in both eyes. NANOS and the American Stroke Association both define HH this way. Verified
The cause is brain-pathway damage, not an eye-only issue. NANOS states the damage is in the brain, and StatPearls describes lesion localization and neuroimaging. Verified

Treatment is supportive: reading/environment, rehab, prisms/scanning. NANOS, American Stroke Association, and the 2025 ESO guideline support screening and compensatory visual scanning/search for visual field loss after stroke. Verified

New hope for visual field loss from stroke

Neuro-ophthalmology discussion of the FLUORESCE pilot trial.

Verified, not actionable

Transcript extraction: the video discusses fluoxetine as a possible early post-stroke visual-recovery adjunct. It correctly frames the result as a small pilot with trend-level findings and a need for larger trials.

0:00Introduces a pilot randomized controlled trial for visual field loss after stroke.

3:00Takeaway: potential signal, but larger and better-controlled cohorts are needed.
Claim Verification Status
There was a pilot randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind trial of fluoxetine for acute ischemic stroke with isolated homonymous hemianopia. The Journal of Neuro-Ophthalmology FLUORESCE paper confirms this design. Verified
The intervention was fluoxetine 20 mg daily for 90 days, started within 10 days of ischemic stroke. Confirmed in the FLUORESCE methods and protocol text. Verified
The primary endpoint showed a trend but was not statistically conclusive. The paper reports nonsignificant benefit on perimetric mean deviation, while concluding the results may inform larger multicenter trial design. Caution
This applies to Eric's chronic 2021 stroke. Not directly. FLUORESCE studied acute ischemic stroke within 10 days; Eric is chronic, with hemorrhagic transformation history, and this is medication territory. Do not apply
Clinical boundary
This is a neurologist-only discussion item, not a NegletFix protocol change. It may belong in the pharmacological-adjuncts watchlist, but it should not influence the Unity AV training module.
