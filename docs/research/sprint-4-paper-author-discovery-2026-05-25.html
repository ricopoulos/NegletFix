2. The Best New Leads

Lead Status Why It Matters NegletFix Decision
Alharshan / Alwashmi / Rowe et al. 2026 NeuroImage Promote Directly matches the current direction: immersive AV training in post-stroke HH, 30 min/day, 5 days/week, 6 weeks, n=15, reaction-time improvement plus DTI/functional-connectivity changes. Use as the current strongest direct bridge from protocol to mechanism. Keep expectations modest: RT/plasticity evidence, not full restoration guarantee.
Fayed et al. 2026 review on post-stroke visual field assessment and emerging tech Promote Fresh review covering perimetry, substitution, eye-movement therapy, restitution, brain stimulation, VR, and wearable technologies. Use as the umbrella roadmap citation for why NegletFix should combine objective testing, VR tasks, and cautious claims.
NCT07317739: bedside screening + early multisensory AV rehab Watch Not-yet-recruiting 2026 study on post-stroke visual-field screening and multisensory AV stimulation. Monitor for protocol and outcomes. This is close to NegletFix's AV branch, but earlier-stage stroke.
NCT07358832: tDCS + audiovisual training Watch Recruiting 2026-2029 trial combining tDCS and AV training for subacute visual field deficits after stroke. Strong future research watch. Do not add stimulation to home protocol without clinician/research setting.
Bowers Lab 2026 head-scan reinforcement training Promote with caution Three 1-hour driving-simulator sessions improved proactive blind-side scanning and blind-side hazard detection in a small single-arm study. Useful for future Quest functional scanning tasks. Not driving clearance and not restorative field recovery.
SEARCH 2025 scanning RCT Caution anchor Large RCT: both visual scanning training and sham groups improved, with no significant between-group difference. Prevents overclaiming generic scanning/search therapy. Measure outcomes; don't assume benefit.
Hemianopia Reading Questionnaire 2024 Promote New self-report tool focused on reading difficulty in homonymous visual field defects. Potential upgrade path for Sprint 3's reading-effort tracker.

Acceptance Rule

Accept a lead only if the post points to a DOI, PMID, ClinicalTrials.gov record, institution, conference abstract, lab page, or guideline. The post itself is a pointer, not evidence.

6. What Changes For NegletFix

Decision Reason Action
Keep AV/VR as the main branch The 2026 NeuroImage study and active 2026 trials reinforce AV/VR as a live research direction. Continue toward Unity smoke test and 30-session AV protocol.
Add a monthly literature watch The field is moving fast enough that a static wiki will become stale. Run PubMed + ClinicalTrials + X query scan monthly; save only traceable links.
Use SEARCH as a humility anchor Large sham-controlled scanning RCT was not clearly positive over sham. Avoid broad claims like "scanning training works." Measure Eric's own data.
Keep stimulation separate tDCS/tACS studies are promising but specialized and clinician/research setting only. Watch, document, and maybe discuss with clinicians; do not build into current home protocol.
Upgrade Sprint 3 later with HRQ The Hemianopia Reading Questionnaire is a more formal reading-function source. Use HRQ concepts to refine the reading-effort tracker after the first few sessions.
