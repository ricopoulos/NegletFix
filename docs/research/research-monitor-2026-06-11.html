Research flow

YouTube, X, LinkedIn, and NeurIPS are part of intake, not proof.

They are useful because they surface patient language, clinician names, trial mentions, AI-method leads, and paper links. They become evidence only after verification.

01 Discover YouTube, X search queries, LinkedIn posts, NeurIPS/OpenReview pages, podcasts, lab pages, and clinician talks generate leads.
02 Verify Claims must resolve to PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, arXiv/DOI/OpenReview, guidelines, or institutional protocol sources.
03 Grade Separate RCTs, feasibility studies, posted registry outcomes, anecdotes, and adjacent-condition material.
04 Map Translate evidence into core, adjunct, watch, or parked lanes with an explicit protocol-impact note.
05 Track Update CSV, wiki, HTML monitor, and Obsidian notes without automatically changing the protocol.

Monitor operations

Static dashboard first, automation second.

The next useful automation is a monthly diff that proposes a review note. It should never auto-change treatment logic.

Monthly review checklist

  • 1Refresh NCT statuses for CTG-001 through CTG-009 and flag newly posted results.
  • 2Search PubMed for AV+tDCS, audiovisual hemianopia, VR visual restoration, and chronic stroke visual field defects.
