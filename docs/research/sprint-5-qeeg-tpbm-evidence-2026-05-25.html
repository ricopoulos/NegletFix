2. QEEG vs EEG

Term Meaning NegletFix Interpretation
EEG Electrical brain activity recorded from electrodes. Clinical EEG starts with raw waveform review, artifact control, and expert interpretation. Muse EEG is a limited consumer signal. Useful for experiments, not clinical mapping.
QEEG Quantitative EEG: computer analysis of EEG signals, often including spectral power, frequency bands, asymmetry, coherence, topographic maps, or comparisons to normative databases. QEEG is analysis of EEG, not a magical new signal. It can generate maps, but those maps still depend on data quality, artifact removal, assumptions, and expert interpretation.
QEEG-guided treatment A practitioner uses QEEG-derived patterns to choose a treatment target or protocol. This is the claim that needs evidence. For QEEG-guided tPBM in chronic stroke hemianopia, it stays unproven.
Guardrail
ACNS guidance treats QEEG as a specialized adjunct requiring qualified acquisition and raw EEG review. For NegletFix, QEEG should never be accepted as a standalone diagnosis, a sales report, or a targeting oracle.

4. Evidence Map

Question Evidence Found Decision
Can tPBM affect brain physiology? Yes, plausible. Human physiology work with 1064-nm laser reports changes in oxygenated hemoglobin and oxidized cytochrome c oxidase. Supported mechanism
Is tPBM being studied for stroke? Yes. Stroke-specific reviews and trials exist. One 2026 chronic ischemic stroke sham-controlled trial found no significant NIHSS advantage, while a 2025 post-stroke cognitive impairment study reported positive cognitive/neuropsychiatric outcomes. Mixed clinical evidence
Does Naeser have real PBM publications? Yes. Naeser has papers in chronic TBI and a chronic stroke aphasia case series with fMRI connectivity changes. Real but adjacent
Is there strong evidence for tPBM restoring homonymous hemianopia? No direct strong evidence found in this sprint. Not promoted
Is QEEG-guided tPBM targeting validated for chronic post-stroke HH? No strong independent evidence found. The available videos are case-level or practitioner-level claims. Parked
Should NegletFix use Muse EEG as a QEEG map? No. Muse is not clinical-grade multi-channel QEEG and cannot replace qualified EEG/QEEG acquisition, artifact review, and interpretation. Do not do

7. If This Branch Reopens

Requirement Why
Target condition must include stroke visual field loss, HH, occipital stroke, or post-chiasmatic visual pathway damage. TBI cognition, aphasia, and Parkinson's are not enough to infer benefit for Eric's left visual field.
Study should be controlled or at least independently replicated. Case reports and device presentations are vulnerable to placebo, regression to the mean, and selection bias.
Dose/device details must be clear: wavelength, power, irradiance, fluence, pulse frequency, placement, schedule, and adverse events. PBM effects are parameter-dependent. "Laser therapy" is too vague.
QEEG methods must include raw EEG review, montage, recording length, artifact handling, normative database, and qualified interpreter. QEEG maps are only as good as the signal and assumptions behind them.
Outcome must include visual-field, contrast, reaction-time, reading, or daily-function measures. General mood or cognition gains do not answer the NegletFix question.
