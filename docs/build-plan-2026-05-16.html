Can you really alternate A and B? — honest answer

Yes, with caveats. There's no published trial that combines the two paradigms, so we're in extrapolation territory. But the underlying mechanisms are different enough that a combined approach is defensible — they likely train complementary capacities rather than competing for the same neural substrate.

Two ways to alternate — pick one

Option 1: Session-by-session (A-B-A-B-A-B)

30 sessions over 6 weeks, alternating paradigms each day. Pro: maximum mechanism diversity per week. Con: both paradigms must be built before session 1 — that's significant dev cost up front, and you can't ship anything testable until both are done. Also: if it works, you can't easily attribute which paradigm drove the gain.

Option 2: Sequential blocks (B first, then A)

Phase 1 = 30 sessions of Paradigm B over 6 weeks. Re-measure. Phase 2 = 30 sessions of Paradigm A over 6 weeks. Re-measure. Total: 12 weeks, ~30 hours.

Pro: build B first (faster ship date, you start training sooner), see if you respond at all before investing in the harder build, clearer attribution. Con: each paradigm gets a full 6-week block which is the right dose anyway, so this is mostly upside.

Recommendation Go sequential. Build Paradigm B first because (a) you have severe baseline deficit and B's scotoma-border detection training is the right entry point per Yang/Cavanaugh/Saionz 2023, (b) build cost is ~3× lower so you actually start training in weeks not months, (c) if you're a non-responder to B, that's important information before investing in the harder A build, (d) clean attribution if either phase produces gains.
Important — within-instrument tracking only All comparisons across phases must be on the same headset with the same display calibration. If you upgrade Quest 2 → Quest 3 between phases, you'll need to re-baseline because of the Zeri 2018 LCD-vs-printed effect. Best timing: acquire the Quest 3 before Phase 1 starts so the entire program runs on one device.

The honest expectation map

Set expectations realistically Based on Yang/Cavanaugh/Saionz 2023 (n=12 chronic V1-stroke patients) and Saionz 2020/2025:
  • ~50–60% chance of any measurable gain at trained locations (you might be a non-responder)
  • If you respond: realistic magnitude is +0.20 to +0.40 LogCS at scotoma-border, not full restoration
  • Blind-field CS will likely remain ~4× lower than intact-field even after training
  • Functional outcomes (RT improvements, scanning efficiency, ADL transfer) may be more meaningful than perimetric field-area gains
  • Your 2.25 LogCS left-right asymmetry will not fully close — this is a fundamental limit of V1-damaged tissue, not a protocol failure

What "success" looks like for this program: a measurable +0.30 LogCS gain at one or more scotoma-border locations, sustained between Phase 2 and Phase 4 endpoints, with corresponding RT improvements visible in the DataLogger trials. That's clinically significant by the Elliott/Bullimore/Bailey 1991 threshold, and it would put you in the responder subgroup of chronic-stroke patients per the Huxlin lab data.

What "doesn't work" looks like, also honestly: 30 sessions of Phase 2 with no detectable shift in any of (Left LogCS at trained locations, mean RT to peripheral stimuli, fixation accuracy). If that happens, Phase 3 (3D-MOT-IVR) is still worth trying because it engages different mechanisms — but expectations should adjust downward.
